Sketch 2: The Foggy Mirror Clock

Context: This sketch visualizes time as a fog on a mirror that must be physically removed to gain clarity. It addresses the user's morning routine by turning the act of mirror-cleaning into a functional temporal tool. It's designed for a bathroom environment during a high-activity morning routine, where time is felt through humidity and hygiene routines.

Design Decisions:

  • I used tooth brushes to represent the hour and minute hands of the clock
  • The tooth brushes "wipe away" the fog on the mirror to reveal a clock
  • Most clocks "add" marks; this clock "subtracts" fog to reveal time, emphasizing that time is often what we find when we clear away the morning haze.

Interactivity: The user can hold their mouse down and erase the fog on the mirror

Future Work: I could integrate a "re-fogging" animation that slowly covers the mirror again as the day progresses, symbolizing the fading of morning clarity.

Sketch 3: The Coffee Foam Clock

Context: This sketch measures an hour through the dissipation of coffee foam. It addresses the need for an ambient time-tracker during moments of rest or focused desk work. It's best used as a "Pomodoro" or break-timer in a kitchen or office setting, where a cup of coffee typically marks a 30-60 minute window of time.

Design Decisions:

  • I used small coffee marks around the rim of the cup to represent how many hours have passed in the day with 24 being present at the end of the day
  • The minute-based foam dissipates as the hour progresses, covering the cup at minute 1 and barely being visible at minute 59.
  • The bubble in the cup pops every second signaling the seconds passing as the coffee sits.

Interactivity: User can click on the foam to swirl it

Future Work: I would like to add a "steam" effect that moves in response to the user's microphone (breathing), making the coffee feel hot and physically present.

Sketch 4: The Melting Ice Cube Clock

Context: This sketch represents the inevitable "melting away" of a work day or a deadline. It addresses the user's need to visualize 12 hours as a decreasing volume of solid matter. It's designed for high-focus environments where the user wants to visualize the "weight" of the time they have remaining in a session.

Design Decisions:

  • Drawing the ice cube in 3D allows the user to judge time through the amount left making the hour feel more substantial than a flat 2D line.
  • The height of the cube represents the hours, and the size of the drip puddle at the bottom represents how many minutes have passed in the hour, resetting each hour
  • The drip moves every second provides a constant reminder of the "leakage" of time, creating a sense of urgency as the water hits the puddle.

Future Work: I would add a "refreeze" animation at midnight where the puddle is sucked back up into a solid block, signifying the start of a fresh day.
